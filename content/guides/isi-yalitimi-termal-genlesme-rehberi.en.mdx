## Problem / Objective

Insulation does more than reduce energy loss; it affects surface temperature, safety, expansion gaps, and connection stresses.

## Assumptions

- Conduction is treated as the dominant heat transfer mode.
- Thermal conductivity is selected at operating temperature.
- Movement freedom or compensation is planned for expansion.

## Step by step method

1. Define temperature difference and contact area.
2. Select conductivity at operating temperature.
3. Check surface temperature with insulation thickness.
4. Calculate thermal expansion.
5. Revise joints and clearances for expansion.

## Common mistakes

- Selecting insulation thickness separately from assembly clearance.
- Forgetting conductivity changes with temperature.
- Fully restraining expansion with fixed joints.

## Quick FAQ

### Question: How is insulation thickness selected?

Energy loss, surface temperature, packaging, and cost are evaluated together.

### Question: Why does thermal expansion matter?

If restrained, it can create extra stress and deformation in joints.

### Question: Is thermal conductivity constant?

For many materials it changes with temperature; check the data sheet range.
