## Calculation Explanation

**Purpose**
Quickly estimate shaft torque from the relationship between power, speed, and mechanical efficiency.

<Formula label="Core relations">
T = 9550 * P / n
P = T * n / 9550
hp = kW * 1.34102
T_eff = T * (η / 100)
</Formula>

**Assumptions**
- The calculation assumes steady-state operation at constant speed.
- Mechanical losses are represented by a single efficiency factor (η).
- Transients, startup behavior, and shock loading are excluded.

**Units**
- P: kW
- n: rpm
- T, T_eff: N·m
- η: %

**Reference**
- DIN 70020
- ISO 3046

## Examples

### Example 1: Torque for a 5.5 kW motor

**Inputs**
- Power: 5.5 kW
- Speed: 1500 rpm
- Mechanical efficiency: 95%

**Expected outputs**
- Torque (ideal): 35.02 N·m
- Torque (efficiency): 33.27 N·m
- Power: 7.38 hp

### Example 2: 12 kW drive

**Inputs**
- Power: 12 kW
- Speed: 1200 rpm
- Mechanical efficiency: 90%

**Expected outputs**
- Torque (ideal): 95.50 N·m
- Torque (efficiency): 85.95 N·m
- Power: 16.09 hp
