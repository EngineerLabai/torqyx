## Calculation Explanation

**Purpose**
Quickly estimate velocity, Reynolds number, friction factor, and pressure loss in a pipe using the Darcy-Weisbach approach.

<Formula label="Darcy-Weisbach and friction factor">
v = 4Q / (π D^2)
Re = ρ v D / μ
f = 0.25 / [log10(ε/(3.7D) + 5.74/Re^0.9)]^2
ΔP = f (L/D) (ρ v^2 / 2)
</Formula>

**Assumptions**
- Flow is single-phase, fully developed, and in a circular pipe.
- Swamee-Jain approximation is used in the turbulent regime.
- Minor losses (fittings, valves, inlets/outlets) are excluded.

**Units**
- ρ: kg/m³
- μ: Pa·s
- Q: m³/s
- D, ε: mm (converted internally to meters)
- L: m
- ΔP: Pa and bar

**Reference**
- Crane TP-410

## Examples

### Example 1: DN50 water line

**Inputs**
- ρ: 998 kg/m³
- μ: 0.001 Pa·s
- Q: 0.01 m³/s
- D: 50 mm
- L: 30 m
- ε: 0.045 mm

**Expected outputs**
- Velocity: 5.09 m/s
- Re: 254138
- f: 0.0205
- ΔP: 159159 Pa (1.592 bar)
- Pump power: 2.27 kW

### Example 2: DN40 short line

**Inputs**
- ρ: 1000 kg/m³
- μ: 0.0012 Pa·s
- Q: 0.005 m³/s
- D: 40 mm
- L: 20 m
- ε: 0.02 mm

**Expected outputs**
- Velocity: 3.98 m/s
- Re: 132629
- f: 0.0197
- ΔP: 78107 Pa (0.781 bar)
- Pump power: 0.56 kW
