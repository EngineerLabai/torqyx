Power factor is the ratio of real power to apparent power.

## Why it matters

It is used in AC motor and facility power quality calculations.

## How it is used

In a design check, first define the relevant load, dimension, material, or flow condition. Then interpret this term by its role in the formula and compare it with limits.


<Formula label="Basic expression">
Power factor = real power / apparent power
</Formula>

<Callout type="info" title="Example">
Low power factor can require higher current for the same real power.
</Callout>
