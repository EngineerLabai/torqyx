Reynolds number is a dimensionless ratio of inertial to viscous forces.

## Why it matters

It is a practical first check for flow regime.

## How it is used

In a design check, first define the relevant load, dimension, material, or flow condition. Then interpret this term by its role in the formula and compare it with limits.


<Formula label="Basic expression">
Re = density x velocity x diameter / dynamic viscosity
</Formula>

<Callout type="info" title="Example">
In pipe flow, low Reynolds suggests laminar behavior and high Reynolds suggests turbulent behavior.
</Callout>
