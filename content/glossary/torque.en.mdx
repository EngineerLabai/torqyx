Torque is the turning effect of a force around an axis.

## Why it matters

It is a core value in bolt tightening, shaft design, and power transmission.

## How it is used

In a design check, first define the relevant load, dimension, material, or flow condition. Then interpret this term by its role in the formula and compare it with limits.


<Formula label="Basic expression">
Torque = Force x radius
</Formula>

<Callout type="info" title="Example">
A 10 N force applied at a 2 m arm creates 20 N m of torque.
</Callout>
